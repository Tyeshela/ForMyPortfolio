SmartWatch

In my presentation I decided to talk about ….a clock/watch. The thing that is not a subject of fashion but always do change with the fashion circle and as the time changes, the appearance of watches change.

Breifly about its history… Watches were developed in the 17th century from spring-powered clocks, which appeared as early as the 14th century. During most of its history the watch was a mechanical device, driven by clockwork, powered by winding a mainspring, and keeping time with an oscillating balance wheel. These are called mechanical watches. Yes, clocks/watches show time, and it’s priceless, but it has become much more useful as smartwatches appeared, and are becoming more and more trendy, useful and interesting with its features and valuable resources. It does show not only time, but can measure blood pressure, heart rate, count steps when you walk and can even hold several apps that are in your smartphone (sms, calls, listening to music, answering text messages, getting directions, GPRS, etc). \ That’s right, smartwatch is a fashion item, but I can say that it’s also an useful and valuable fashion item.

Early years

The first digital watch, which debuted in 1972, was the Pulsar manufactured by Hamilton Watch Company."Pulsar" became a brand name which would later be acquired by Seiko in 1978.

1990s

The Timex Datalink wristwatch, was introduced in 1994. The early Timex Datalink Smartwatches realized a wireless data transfer mode to receive data from a PC.

2000s

In June 2000, IBM displayed a prototype for the WatchPad, a wristwatch that ran Linux. The original version had only 6 hours of battery life, which was later extended to 12.

2020s

In 2020, the United States Food and Drug Administration granted marketing approval for an Apple Watch app called NightWare.
